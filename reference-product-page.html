Craftsmanship Story +

Hand-embroidered by the karigars of Jaipur using traditional zardozi work — gold and silver thread laid over hand-dyed silk, a technique dating back to the Mughal courts. Each lehenga takes roughly 210 hours to complete.

Fabric & Origin +

Pure mulberry silk base, sourced from Karnataka sericulture farms. Zari thread is 92.5% silver, gold-plated. Dupatta finished with hand-tied tassels.

Size & Fit +

Runs true to size with a fitted blouse and flared skirt. Custom tailoring available at no extra cost — submit measurements after checkout.

Care Instructions +

Dry clean only. Store folded in muslin cloth away from direct sunlight. Avoid contact with perfume or water on embroidered areas.

Sustainability +

Made to order to avoid overproduction. Packaged in a reusable silk box with biodegradable filler. Artisans paid above fair-trade benchmark wages.

Meet the Artisan

Zardozi by the karigars of Jaipur.

This lehenga was embroidered by a collective of 14 karigars led by master craftsman Iqbal Mirza, whose family has practiced zardozi needlework for four generations in the old city of Jaipur.

Every motif is drawn by hand before a single thread is laid — no two lehengas in this edition are stitched in quite the same rhythm.
